# AI search engines for airline discovery and booking

## Core takeaway

AI search is compressing the flight planning journey. Travelers increasingly ask ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and similar systems for curated flight options, route advice and booking guidance instead of browsing many separate result pages. For airlines, GEO is not only about brand awareness; it is about being cited during high-intent travel decisions before the customer reaches a booking path.

The source reports that AI-generated traffic to airline pages is still a small share of total organic traffic, but is growing quickly and shows stronger booking intent. This makes AI search an early but strategically important direct-channel opportunity.

## Why this matters for airlines

Traditional search often separates inspiration, comparison and booking across multiple queries. AI search combines those stages into one conversational answer. If an airline is absent from that answer, or present without a link, the booking path can move to an OTA, aggregator or competitor.

For a single-client airline app, this means recommendations should prioritize:

- city-to-city route visibility;
- non-branded route queries;
- content that answers traveler intent directly;
- AI referral tracking;
- crawler access and page indexability.

## Recommended actions for airline GEO

### 1. Audit LLM crawlability

Airline pages should not accidentally block AI search crawlers or crawlers used by answer engines. The audit should include:

- `robots.txt`;
- edge and firewall rules;
- bot mitigation tools;
- JavaScript or CAPTCHA challenges;
- status codes returned to crawlers;
- whether important route, fare and FAQ pages are accessible without a logged-in session.

### 2. Enrich semantic route content

Route pages should include traveler-focused details that AI systems can summarize and cite:

- flight duration;
- airport names and codes;
- direct vs connecting options;
- route facts;
- terminal or airport guidance when stable;
- baggage rules relevant to the route;
- check-in and boarding guidance;
- policies for changes, cancellations and refunds.

### 3. Track AI referral and intent metrics

Airlines should not wait for perfect GEO analytics. Start with proxy metrics:

- referrals from ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini and Copilot where available;
- landing pages receiving AI traffic;
- flight search initiation after AI referral;
- conversion or booking flow entry from AI traffic;
- prompts where the brand is cited or omitted.

### 4. Monitor city-to-city and non-branded queries

Non-branded route queries are especially important because they reveal organic AI visibility, not just recognition of the airline name.

Examples for Spain and Colombia:

- "flights from Madrid to Bogota";
- "best airline for Spain to Colombia with checked baggage";
- "direct flights from Barcelona to Bogota";
- "airlines flying Madrid Medellin";
- "which airline is best for changing a flight to Colombia";

## Application to recommendations

Use this source to support recommendations when:

- AI/referral traffic is low or not tracked;
- route pages are thin or missing;
- prompts are mostly branded and do not measure real discovery;
- the airline appears in answers but does not receive citations;
- support and operational content is not available in crawlable HTML.

Recommended categories:

- `visibility`;
- `sources`;
- `content`;
- `prompts`.

## Spain and Colombia focus

For this client, the first implementation priority should be Spain-origin routes and Spain customer expectations, followed by Colombia routes and passenger needs.

High-value route/intent combinations:

- Madrid-Bogota discovery and comparison;
- Barcelona-Bogota route availability;
- Spain-Colombia baggage and fare inclusions;
- changes and refunds for Spain-Colombia flights;
- cancellations, delays and compensation from Spain;
- connecting itineraries between Spain and Colombian cities.
